## Core Interaction

### Turn. Press. Know.

**01 – Turn**
Rotate the dial to adjust. Brightness, volume, temperature – whatever your bank controls.

**02 – Press**
A short press toggles or activates. Double press turns Pivot off.

**03 – Know**
The LED ring shows state at a glance – colour-coded by mode, lit by value.

---

## Colour-coded banks

### One dial. Four assignable banks.

Pivot provides four assignable banks, each linked to an entity, scene or script in Home Assistant. Hold and turn the dial to move between banks. The LED ring shows which bank is active, along with the current value.

**Banks:**
- Bank 1 – HomePod – 70%
- Bank 2 – Lamp – 60%
- Bank 3 – Thermostat – 30°
- Bank 4 – Timer – 15m

## The Pivot ecosystem

The Pivot ecosystem is made up of four parts, each designed to complement one another.

Start with the Pivot Dial as a refined enclosure for your VPE, install the firmware and integration for custom control, or bring them together for the full experience.

### Firmware – for richer on-device control
Pivot firmware expands what the Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition (VPE) can do on-device, adding colour-coded banks, tactile knob interaction, LED feedback, and event firing into Home Assistant. It's built on ESPHome, and uses the original Nabu Casa firmware as its foundation.

### Integration – for easy setup and configuration in Home Assistant
The Pivot integration bridges Pivot firmware and Home Assistant, connecting each bank to the entities, scenes and scripts already in your setup. It also provides controls for audio feedback, colours, display behaviour and device preferences.

### Dashboard – for visual feedback and UI support
The dashboard adds an optional visual layer to Pivot, offering a clearer UI for those who want the physical and digital parts of the system to be easier to read and digest.

### Hardware – for a more functional physical form
The Pivot Dial gives the VPE a more considered form, designed around touch, clearer microphone pickup and placement within the home.

## Hardware

### Tactile control – The VPE enclosure, reimagined.

Pivot Dial reshapes the Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition around the way it's used within the Pivot ecosystem, with the dial brought forward for control, the LED ring more clearly defined for glanceable feedback, an upright design for clearer microphone pickup and more natural placement in the home.

**Features:**
- **Dial** – Combines the dial and button into one tactile control. Turn to adjust, press to select.
- **Diffused LED ring** – 12-segment feedback channels hidden beneath the printed surface for a cleaner face.
- **Mount** – Desk, shelf, bench or wall mount options, with the upright desk mount helping conceal the cable. *(Right-angle USB-C adapter required)*

---

### Placement and orientation – A better position for everyday control.

Pivot Dial offers two body styles and a set of mount options, so the VPE can sit where it makes the most sense for your space.

The upright body provides a more visible, angled control point with cleaner cable concealment, while the shortened body suits a lower-profile desk or wall placement.

The firmware also lets you set the LED orientation to match the body and mount, so the light ring displays the right way up in any position.

**Mount options:**
- **Upright desk mount** – An angled form that brings the dial forward and helps conceal the cable behind the body.
- **Low-profile mount** – A shorter body and mount combo for desks or walls where a flatter, more minimal footprint might make sense.
- **Wall mount** – A flat mounting option for vertical placement, designed to work with the shorter body.

---

### Customisable – Printed your way.

Pivot Dial is made to be 3D printed, giving you more control over how the hardware looks and feels in your space.

Keep it quiet and minimal, give the dial a stronger presence, or choose a mount / body style that better suits your room. Differences in colour, material or placement can change the character of the device without changing how it works.

Because the files can be modified, Pivot Dial can keep evolving, whether you want to refine a detail, adjust the form, or adapt it to your own setup.

**Customisation options:**
- **Body** – Choose the body style that suits how and where Pivot Dial will be used.
- **Colour** – Explore subtle or contrasting combinations across the enclosure, dial and mount.
- **Remix** – Edit the STL files to refine details, adjust the form or adapt Pivot Dial to your setup.

## Pivot use cases

### Quick control for everyday moments.

- **Lights** – Dim, brighten and toggle lights from one physical control, with LED feedback that can also mirror RGB colour for supported lights.
- **Media** – Adjust volume instantly without opening an app or speaking over the room, with clear LED feedback as you turn.
- **Climate** – Raise or lower temperature from a single tactile control, with optional colour feedback to make changes easier to read.
- **Scenes** – Trigger scenes, routines and presets with a single press, so the moments you use most are always within reach.
- **Timers** – Set, start and pause timers directly from the device, with LED ring progress and full visibility in Home Assistant.
- **Scripts & Custom Automations** – Assign turns and presses to scripts or automations, unlocking anything Home Assistant can control.

## Transparent by design

### Know what you're installing.

Pivot is built for people who care how their smart home works. It's local, ESPHome-based, openly documented, and designed to keep the existing Voice Preview Edition functionality as intact as possible.

The firmware builds on the official Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition ESPHome configuration, while the integration and dashboard remain separate layers you can inspect, modify, or safely remove at any time.

**Proof points:**
- **Local communication** – Only communicates with Home Assistant through ESPHome.
- **VPE functionality preserved** – Wake word, voice interaction and the existing ESPHome integration remain a core part of the experience.
- **Reversible setup** – Firmware installation can be rolled back, and the software layers can be safely removed at any time.
- **Open documentation** – Source files, setup steps and architecture are documented so the system stays transparent.
