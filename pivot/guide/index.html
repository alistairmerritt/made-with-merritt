Before you start

Get set up.

Before you begin, make sure you have everything prepared. Assembly is straightforward, but taking a moment to get set up will make the process smoother.

What you'll need

  • Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition
  • 3D printer or access to a print service
  • PLA or similar filament
  • Right-angle USB-C adapter  (Stand Build only)
  • If you wish to use an external speaker with the Stand Build, a right-angle 3.5mm adapter will also be required.

Before you assemble

Pivot Dial involves carefully re-housing the Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition. The process is straightforward when done with care, but it does require removing the VPE from its original enclosure. Take your time and avoid forcing any components.

Build options

Choose your build.

Pivot Dial is available in two configurations, depending on how you want it to sit, feel and be assembled.

Option 1

Stand Build

The cleanest option for desks, shelves and benches, designed to conceal the cable with the Desk Stand and a right-angle USB-C adapter.

  • Desk, shelf or bench placement
  • Concealed cable with Desk Stand
  • Requires right-angle USB-C adapter

Option 2

Flat Build

A lower-profile option for wall mounting, upright surfaces, or flatter desk placement, with a visible rear-exiting cable.

  • Wall mounting
  • Upright surfaces
  • Low-profile desk placement
  • No right-angle adapter needed

Note

The Stand Build requires a right-angle USB-C adapter. A standard straight USB-C cable will not fit within the enclosure. If connecting to an external speaker via the 3.5mm output, a right-angle AUX adapter can also help with cable routing.

Each body configuration includes the same core assembly system. The difference is in body depth, backplate length and cable-routing position.

Mount options

Place it your way.

Choose a mount that suits where and how you'll use Pivot Dial.

Desk mount

An angled mount designed primarily for the Stand Build. The cleanest option for desks and shelves, especially with a right-angle USB-C adapter.

Raised desk mount

A lower-profile mount for desks, or fixed to a wall at a slight angle.

Flat wall mount

Designed for vertical surfaces. Works best with the Flat Build.

Printing & assembly

Build notes

A few things to know before you start printing, and what to expect during assembly.

Printing

Yes. The main shell and backplate require supports. Remove these before starting assembly.

The face / cover should be printed with no infill so the LED ring can shine through the surface. All other parts can use standard infill settings.

PLA works well and is recommended. For the face / cover, grey, bone or soft neutral filaments give the best LED definition. Pure white can over-diffuse the light. Translucent PLA also works well for a more see-through look.

Print on a smooth bed for the cleanest finish on visible parts. No sanding should be required if parts come out cleanly.

Assembly

Yes. Pivot Dial involves re-housing the Voice Preview Edition. You'll need to carefully disassemble the original VPE shell before fitting the hardware into the Pivot Dial body. Full instructions are in the guide.

Refer to the full written guide for a complete hardware list with specific screw sizes and quantities for your chosen build.

Only for the Stand Build. A standard straight cable won't fit within the enclosure. The Flat Build doesn't require one — the cable exits from the side of the body.

The dial may take some adjustment to seat correctly. Rotate and adjust slowly until it moves cleanly in both directions — up/down press and clockwise/counterclockwise rotation. Take your time with this step.

Pivot Dial requires removing the Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition from its original enclosure. This may affect your original product warranty, especially if any damage occurs during disassembly or reassembly. Take your time, avoid forcing components, and follow the full guide carefully.

Firmware

The enclosure
is just the start.

Pivot Dial works out of the box as an enclosure for the Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition.

To unlock banks, scenes, timers and the full Pivot control experience, you'll need to install the Pivot firmware and integration separately. It's ESPHome-based, openly documented, and doesn't break existing VPE functionality.

Required separately

Firmware is not included with the printed parts and must be installed by the user. Full instructions are in the Pivot documentation.
