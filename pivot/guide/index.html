Pivot Dial

Printing & Assembly Guide

This guide covers the printable parts, body options, mount options, printing notes and basic assembly considerations for Pivot Dial.

What you'll need

Before you begin, make sure you have everything on hand.

Required

  • Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition
  • 3D printer or access to a print service
  • PLA or similar filament
  • Right-angle USB-C adapter (required for the upright body)
  • Optional: right-angle 3.5mm adapter for an external speaker connection

Body options

Pivot Dial includes two body configurations. Choose the one that best suits your placement and setup.

Option 1

Upright body

The main version — best for desks, bedside tables, and dining areas. A lower "chin" section mostly conceals the cable when used with the desk mount and a right-angle USB-C adapter.

  • Desk or shelf placement
  • Cleaner cable concealment
  • More sculptural presence

Option 2

Short body

No chin. Designed for lower-profile placement or wall mounting. The cable exits from the side, so it will remain visible.

  • Wall mounting
  • Low-profile desk placement
  • No right-angle adapter needed

Note

The upright body requires a right-angle USB-C adapter. A standard straight USB-C cable will not fit within the enclosure.

Mount options

Three mount options are available depending on how and where you want to place the dial.

Desk mount An angled mount designed primarily for the upright body. Cleanest option for desks and shelves, especially with a right-angle USB-C adapter.
Raised desk mount A lower-profile mount for desks or fixed to a wall for a slight angle.
Flat wall mount Designed for vertical surfaces. Works best with the short body.

Included parts

Each body configuration includes the same core assembly system. The difference is in body depth, backplate length and cable-routing position.

Upright body

  • Dial outer
  • Dial inner
  • Face / cover
  • Main shell
  • Backplate

Short body

  • Dial outer
  • Dial inner
  • Short face / cover
  • Short main shell
  • Short backplate

On the upright body, cable management runs through the backplate. On the short body, cable routing runs through the main shell.

Printing notes

Supports

The main shell and backplate require supports. Remove these after printing before assembly.

Face / cover

The face should be printed with no infill so the LED ring can shine through the printed surface.

Filament tip

Grey, bone, beige or soft neutral filaments give the best LED definition. Pure white can diffuse light too much, making the LEDs less distinct. Translucent PLA also works well for a more minimal, see-through look.

Print surface

For the cleanest finish, print on a smooth print bed. This gives the best surface finish for the visible parts of the enclosure.

Post-processing

No sanding should be required if the parts are printed cleanly.

Cable notes

Upright body

Requires a right-angle USB-C adapter. Without one, the cable will not fit within the enclosure. When used with the desk mount, the cable can be mostly hidden.

Short body

No right-angle adapter required. The cable exits from the side and will remain visible. This is the better option if you want to avoid buying an adapter, or if you're mounting to a wall or upright surface.

Optional audio adapter

If connecting the VPE to an external speaker via the 3.5mm output, a right-angle AUX adapter can help with cable routing.

Dial assembly

The dial assembly may take some trial and error. When fitting the dial, you may need to adjust, rotate and test the position until you find the correct grooves.

Once aligned properly, the dial should allow both up/down button movement and clockwise/counterclockwise rotation.

Take your time

The correct fit may not be obvious immediately. With small adjustments you should find a position where the dial moves cleanly in both directions.

Recommended configuration

Two setups, depending on how you plan to use Pivot Dial.

Cleanest desk setup

  • Upright body
  • Desk mount
  • Right-angle USB-C adapter
  • Grey, bone or beige face — no infill
  • Smooth print bed finish

Wall mounting

  • Short body
  • Flat wall mount
  • Side-exiting cable
  • Optional firmware LED orientation adjustment

Firmware

Pivot Dial can be used as a physical enclosure for the Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition on its own.

To unlock the full Pivot control experience — banks, scenes, timers and more — you'll need to install the optional Pivot firmware and integration separately.

Required separately

Firmware is not included with the printed parts and must be installed by the user. Full instructions are in the Pivot documentation.
