Pivot Dial

Full Assembly
Guide

This guide walks through removing the Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition from its original enclosure and reassembling it inside Pivot Dial.

Allow around 20 minutes once your printed parts are ready. Take your time, avoid forcing any components, and follow each section carefully.

Warranty note

Pivot Dial requires removing the Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition from its original enclosure. This process may affect your original product warranty, especially if damage occurs during disassembly or reassembly. Take your time and avoid forcing any parts.

Before you begin

What you'll need

Gather everything before starting. Having the right tools and parts to hand will make the process smoother.

Required

  • Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition
  • Printed Pivot Dial parts STL files or prepared 3MF file
  • No. 1 and No. 2 crosshead screwdriver
  • Small flathead screwdriver
  • USB-C cable
  • Right-angle USB-C adapter Stand Build only
  • Clean workspace
  • Small tray or container for screws

Keep screws organised

The VPE uses several different screw sizes. Keep them separated as you go — particularly the smaller speaker screws, which are easy to lose and difficult to source if misplaced.

Section 1

Disassembly

Remove the Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition from its original enclosure. Work carefully and keep all parts to hand — everything removed here will be reused.

01

Remove rubber pads and rear screws

Flip the VPE over and peel back the four rubber feet. Beneath each pad you'll find a screw. Remove all four screws using a No. 2 crosshead screwdriver and set them aside with the pads.

Take care

Peel the rubber pads back slowly to avoid tearing. They can be re-applied after disassembly if needed.

02

Lift away the original top enclosure

With the rear screws removed, the top shell can be lifted away from the base. Gently separate the two halves, starting from the rear edge. There are no clips — the shell should come apart cleanly once the screws are out.

Take care

Do not force the shell apart. If it resists, check that all four rear screws have been fully removed.

03

Remove the PCB screws

With the top shell removed, locate the screws securing the PCB to the base. Remove them carefully and set aside. Do not disconnect any cables at this stage.

04

Remove the speaker

Locate the speaker at the rear of the unit. It is secured with small screws — note that these are smaller than the enclosure screws and should be stored separately. Gently disconnect the speaker cable and set the speaker aside.

Take care

The speaker screws are small and easy to lose. Move them to a separate section of your tray immediately after removal.

05

Remove the mute switch

The mute switch is located on the side of the unit. Carefully detach it from its position and disconnect from the PCB. Note how it is oriented before removing — this will make reassembly easier.

06

Remove the LED diffuser ring

The LED diffuser ring sits around the dial area and clips or screws into place. Carefully unclip or unscrew it and set aside. This component will be transferred directly into the Pivot Dial front face during reassembly.

07

Remove the dial and centre button

Carefully lift the dial assembly away from the PCB. The centre button is a separate component — take note of how it sits relative to the dial before removing. The rubber ring around the button should stay with the button.

Note

Take a photo of the dial orientation before removing if you're unsure. The rubber ring on the centre button must be kept — it will be transferred to the new Pivot Dial button during reassembly.

08

Confirm all parts are ready

Before moving to assembly, lay out all components and check nothing is missing. You should have: the PCB, speaker and speaker screws, mute switch, LED diffuser ring, dial, centre button with rubber ring, and all original enclosure screws.

Section 2

Assembly

Reassemble the hardware inside the Pivot Dial enclosure. Follow each step in order. Take extra care when fitting the dial and button — they may take a little adjustment to seat correctly.

01

Prepare the printed Pivot Dial parts

Check that all printed parts are clean and free from support material. Remove any supports or stringing before starting. The front face should have no infill — this allows the LED ring to shine through the surface evenly.

Before you continue

Do not proceed with parts that have leftover support material in mounting holes or recesses — this can prevent components from seating correctly.

02

Transfer the rubber ring to the new button

Carefully remove the rubber ring from the original VPE centre button and transfer it onto the Pivot Dial centre button. The ring should seat cleanly into the groove without needing to be stretched or forced.

03

Assemble the button and dial

Place the centre button into the dial assembly. It should move freely before being fitted to the PCB. Do not force the dial onto the PCB at this stage — this will be done once the PCB is seated in the body.

04

Fit the LED diffuser ring

Place the LED diffuser ring into the front face of the Pivot Dial body. It should sit flush with the surface and click or seat into position. Do not force it — if it resists, check the orientation.

05

Attach the speaker

Position the speaker into the speaker recess in the Pivot Dial body. Secure it using the small speaker screws removed during disassembly. Reconnect the speaker cable to the PCB.

Take care

Do not overtighten the speaker screws — the speaker housing can crack under excessive force.

06

Attach the mute switch

Fit the mute switch into the mute switch recess in the Pivot Dial body and reconnect it to the PCB. Ensure it is correctly oriented so the toggle is accessible through the opening in the shell.

07

Place the PCB into the Pivot Dial body

Lower the PCB into the Pivot Dial body, aligning it with the mounting points. Ensure the USB-C port aligns with the cable exit in the enclosure. For the Stand Build, the port should face downward toward the cable channel.

Stand Build note

The Stand Build requires a right-angle USB-C adapter. Route the cable before securing the PCB — it is much harder to fit once the enclosure is closed.

08

Secure the PCB

Once the PCB is correctly seated, secure it using the PCB screws removed during disassembly. Tighten gently — the PCB does not need to be under heavy tension.

09

Fit the front face

Align the front face over the body, passing the dial and button through the dial opening. The LED ring window should sit directly over the LED diffuser ring. Press down gently and evenly until the face seats flush with the body.

Take care

Do not press down on the dial itself when fitting the front face. Apply pressure to the edges of the face panel only.

10

Close the enclosure

Align the backplate with the rear of the body and secure it using the original VPE enclosure screws. Work through each screw progressively rather than fully tightening one at a time — this helps the plate seat evenly.

Take care

Do not overtighten. The printed body can crack if screws are driven in too hard. Tighten until snug, then stop.

11

Attach the selected mount

Attach your chosen mount to the rear of the Pivot Dial body using the mount fixings. Ensure the mount is correctly aligned with the mounting points before securing. All three mount types attach at the same rear interface.

12

Connect USB-C power

For the Stand Build, connect the right-angle USB-C adapter first, routing the cable down through the stand channel. For the Flat Build, connect directly. Once connected, power on and confirm the device starts normally.

All done

Check everything is working.

Once assembled, connect Pivot Dial to USB-C power and confirm the device starts normally. The LED ring should illuminate and the dial should respond to rotation and press.

Dial adjustment

If the dial feels stiff or doesn't respond cleanly, open the enclosure and re-seat the dial assembly. It may need slight rotation to find the correct position. Take your time — do not force it.
