## Full Assembly Guide

This guide walks through removing the Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition from its original enclosure and reassembling it inside Pivot Dial.

Allow around 20 minutes once your printed parts are ready. Take your time, avoid forcing any components, and follow each section carefully.

> **Care note:** Pivot Dial requires removing the Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition from its original enclosure. This process may affect your original product warranty, especially if damage occurs during disassembly or reassembly. Take your time, avoid forcing any parts, and work on a clean, stable surface.

## Before you begin – What you'll need

Gather everything before starting. Having the right tools and parts to hand will make the process smoother.

### Required

- Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition
- Printed Pivot Dial parts *(STL files or prepared 3MF file)*
- No. 1 crosshead screwdriver
- No. 2 crosshead screwdriver
- USB-C cable
- Right-angle USB-C adapter *(Stand Build only)*
- Right-angle AUX adapter *(if required for your build)*

### Recommended

- Clean workspace
- Small flathead screwdriver
- Small tray or container for screws
- Container for discarded VPE parts *(in case you want to reassemble your VPE in the future)*

> **Keep screws organised:** Keep the screws organised as you work, especially the smaller speaker screws from the original enclosure. They are easy to misplace.

> **Handle the PCB carefully:** Take care when handling the PCB. Hold it by the edges where possible and avoid touching the components directly.

## Section 1 – Disassembly

Remove the Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition from its original enclosure. Work carefully and keep all parts to hand – some components will be reused.

---

### Step 01 – Remove rubber pads and rear screws

Flip the VPE over and peel back the four rubber feet. Beneath each pad you'll find a screw. Remove all four screws using a No. 2 crosshead screwdriver and set them aside with the pads.

---

### Step 02 – Lift away the original top enclosure

With the rear screws removed, the top shell can be lifted away from the base. Gently separate the two halves, starting from the rear edge. There are no clips – the shell should come apart cleanly once the screws are out.

> **Take care:** Do not force the shell apart. If it resists, check that all four rear screws have been fully removed.

---

### Step 03 – Remove the PCB screws

With the top shell removed, locate the screws securing the PCB to the base. Remove them carefully and set aside. Do not disconnect any cables at this stage.

> **Important:** These are the only screws you'll need to reassemble the Pivot Dial. Keep them somewhere safe and separate from the other VPE hardware.

---

### Step 04 – Remove the speaker

Locate the speaker at the rear of the unit. It is secured with small screws – note that these are smaller than the enclosure screws. Gently disconnect the speaker cable and set the speaker aside.

---

### Step 05 – Remove the mute switch

The mute switch is located on the side of the unit. Carefully detach it from its position and disconnect from the PCB.

---

### Step 06 – Remove the LED diffuser ring

The LED diffuser ring sits around the dial area and clips or screws into place. Carefully unclip or unscrew it and set aside.

---

### Step 07 – Remove the dial and centre button

Carefully lift the dial assembly away from the PCB. The centre button is a separate component – take note of how it sits relative to the dial before removing. The rubber ring around the button should stay with the button.

> **Note:** Take a photo of the dial orientation before removing if you're unsure. The rubber ring on the centre button must be kept – it will be transferred to the new Pivot Dial button during reassembly.

---

### Step 08 – Confirm all parts are ready for reassembly

Before moving to assembly, confirm you have the components needed for reassembly:

- PCB
- Speaker
- Dial centre button with rubber ring
- PCB screws (set aside in step 03)

All other parts – the original enclosure, rubber pads, mute switch, LED diffuser ring, and remaining screws – can be discarded or stored safely in case you want to reassemble your VPE in the future.

## Section 2 – Assembly

Reassemble the hardware inside the Pivot Dial enclosure. Follow each step in order.

---

### Step 01 – Prepare the printed Pivot Dial parts

Check each printed part before assembly. Remove any remaining support material, loose filament or rough edges. You may need to shave away excess support material using a sharp knife or similar tool.

Pay particular attention to the main body, faceplate, backplate, speaker cavity, mute switch opening and mount groove.

> **Note:** If the faceplate, backplate or mount does not sit flush later in the assembly, remaining support material is the most likely cause.

---

### Step 02 – Fit the mute switch to the PCB

Place the mute switch onto the PCB, making sure it is facing the correct way up. Check the orientation before moving to the next step, as the switch needs to align with the opening in the Pivot Dial body.

---

### Step 03 – Connect right-angle adapters, if required

If you are using the Stand Build, connect the right-angle USB-C adapter to the PCB before placing the PCB into the body. If your build uses an aux connection, connect the optional right-angle aux adapter at this stage as well.

> **Note:** It is much easier to connect these adapters before the PCB is seated inside the enclosure.

---

### Step 04 – Place the PCB into the main body

Carefully angle the PCB into the main body, with the mute switch entering first. Guide the mute switch through its opening, then gently lower the rest of the PCB into place.

The fit may feel a little tight, but the PCB should slide in without being forced. Once seated, the PCB should sit as flat and flush as possible inside the body.

> **Take care:** Do not force the PCB down. If it does not sit correctly, remove it and check for support material or obstruction inside the printed body.

---

### Step 05 – Click the faceplate into the main body

With the PCB seated, click the faceplate into the main body. It may take a firm press to seat the faceplate fully. Make sure it sits squarely and flush against the main body.

If the faceplate does not sit properly, remove it and check the inside edges of the main body for excess support material. Carefully shave or clean away any obstruction before trying again.

---

### Step 06 – Secure the PCB to the faceplate

Screw the PCB into the faceplate using the PCB screws set aside during disassembly. This holds the PCB, faceplate and main body together to form the front enclosure. An electric screwdriver can help, but use it carefully.

> **Take care:** Tighten the screws enough to hold the faceplate securely, but do not overtighten. Overtightening may damage the PCB or printed parts.

---

### Step 07 – Fit the speaker into the backplate

Slide the speaker into the speaker cavity in the backplate. The fit is designed to be snug and should hold the speaker without screws. It may need a firm press, but avoid forcing it.

Make sure any support material has been removed from the speaker cavity before fitting the speaker.

---

### Step 08 – Connect the speaker wire

Connect the speaker wire to the PCB. Check that the wire is secure and positioned so it will not be pinched when the backplate is installed.

---

### Step 09 – Fit the backplate into the main body

Slide the backplate into the main body. It should click into place if the tolerances are correct and the printed parts are clean. Check that the backplate sits flat and that the speaker openings remain clear.

---

### Step 10 – Attach the inner dial

Turn the device over so the backplate is flat on the table. Align the inner dial with the black dial attached to the PCB, then press it down until it clicks into place. This may require a firm press. You should feel or hear it seat properly.

---

### Step 11 – Fit the outer dial

Take the outer dial and align the teeth around its outside with the four guiding indents in the faceplate. Slide the outer dial over the inner dial. This may take a little trial and error because the dial needs to support both rotation and button press movement.

Without power connected, press the button a few times and rotate the dial. The button should press cleanly, and the dial should turn smoothly.

If the button feels too stiff, remove the outer dial, rotate it slightly and try another groove. Repeat until the button press feels reliable and the rotation feels natural.

---

### Step 12 – Attach the selected mount

Hold your selected mount against the flat section of the backplate, opposite the speaker holes. Slide the mount into place until it feels tight and secure. The groove should help lock the mount in position.

---

### Step 13 – Connect the USB-C cable

If you are using the Stand Build, feed the USB-C cable through the hole in the stand and connect it to the right-angle USB-C adapter. Check that the cable sits naturally and is not placing unnecessary pressure on the PCB or adapter.

---

## All done – Check everything is working.

Connect the Pivot Dial to USB-C power and confirm the device starts normally. Check each of the following before finishing.

**Checklist:**
- The device powers on
- The LED ring lights correctly
- The dial turns smoothly
- The button presses cleanly
- The mute switch moves correctly
- The speaker is seated securely
- The mount feels stable

> **If something doesn't feel right:** If anything feels stiff, loose or misaligned, disconnect power and inspect the relevant part before continuing.
